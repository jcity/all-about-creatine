Overview

Thorne has built its reputation on pharmaceutical-grade supplements, and its creatine is no exception. This is a single-ingredient product: 5g of creatine monohydrate per scoop, nothing else. What sets it apart is the verification behind it — the product is NSF Certified for Sport, meaning each batch is independently tested for over 200 substances banned in competitive sport, plus label accuracy and contaminants.1

The creatine itself is Creapure®, manufactured in Germany under tightly controlled conditions and widely considered the purest monohydrate on the market. For the science on why monohydrate is the form to choose, see our complete guide to creatine.

Quick take: If you compete in a tested sport, this is the obvious choice. If you don't, you're paying a premium for peace of mind — which may or may not be worth it to you.

How it performed in testing

Mixability

We mixed a scoop into 10oz of cold water and a separate scoop into a protein shake. In water it dissolved within about 15 seconds of stirring, leaving only a faint trace of grit at the bottom that disappeared on a second stir. In the shake it was undetectable. This is among the best mixability we've tested in a non-micronized product.

Taste & texture

Genuinely flavorless and odorless. There's no metallic aftertaste that some cheaper creatines carry, and no clumping in the tub over our two-week trial.

Tolerance

At the standard 5g maintenance dose, no testers reported bloating or GI discomfort. As with any monohydrate, those especially sensitive may prefer an HCl form — but most people tolerate this well.2
