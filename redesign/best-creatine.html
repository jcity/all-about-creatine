How we tested

We evaluated more than 20 creatine supplements over a minimum two-week trial each, scoring every product against five weighted criteria:

Purity & ingredients — what's actually in it, and whether there are needless fillers.
Third-party testing — independent checks for banned substances and label accuracy.
Mixability — does it dissolve cleanly or leave gritty residue?
Value — price per serving relative to overall quality.
Brand reputation — track record, transparency, and support.

Frequently asked questions

Do I really need an expensive creatine?

No. Creatine is creatine. A budget option like Nutricost delivers the same results as a premium pick — the difference is in third-party certifications, mixability, and brand trust.

Should I get flavored or unflavored?

Unflavored is generally better. It's more versatile (mix it with anything) and avoids artificial sweeteners and flavors.

Is Creapure worth the premium?

Creapure is made in Germany under strict quality controls. If purity is a top priority — especially for tested athletes — it's worth it. For most recreational users, any reputable brand works.
