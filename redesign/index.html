Design system at a glance

The goal: trade the generic SaaS-template look (gradient hero, three rounded feature cards, newsletter capture, no credibility signals) for a calm, clinical, health-authority aesthetic that reads like a trustworthy medical resource — the kind of look that earns reader trust and, in turn, affiliate clicks.

Color palette

Navy #0e2a47
Teal #0d8f8f
Mint #e7f4f3
Gold #f5b301
Amber CTA #f2a414
Off-white #f6f9fb

Clinical navy + teal carry the "medical credibility" tone; gold/amber is reserved for affiliate "Check price" buttons so the money actions stand out without cheapening the page. Generous white space throughout.

Typography

Source Serif 4 for headlines (an editorial, journal-like authority) paired with Inter for body and UI (clean, legible, modern). Both loaded from Google Fonts.

Trust components introduced

  • Navy trust strip (dietitian-reviewed, studies cited, no paid rankings)
  • "Medically reviewed by Dr. ___, RD" bylines with reviewer cards
  • Cited claims with superscript reference links + a References section
  • Clear, repeated affiliate disclosures (header callout + footer)
  • Scored comparison tables, star ratings, rating-breakdown bars, pros/cons, verdict boxes
  • Amber affiliate CTAs and a mobile sticky "Check price" bar
  • "Last updated / verified" dates and a "How we tested" methodology block

Full rationale is in DESIGN_NOTES.md. Every mockup is a self-contained, responsive HTML file (inline CSS, Google Fonts only — no other dependencies) and the four pages are cross-linked so you can click between them.
